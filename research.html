Research

X-Ray Phase Contrast Imaging

X-ray imaging is widely utilized to view the interior structure of objects. Transmission imaging, however, is generally unable to distinguish between similarly attenuationg materials. X-ray phase contrast imaging, which measures the change in phase through an object rather than the attenuation, produces higher contrast images. Current phase imaging methods generally require complicated setups or expensive equipment. My work focuses on designing novel methods for phase imaging that are more accessible to aviation security and medical imaging applications.

Simulation

Simulation is a powerful tool for modelling x-ray imaging systems prior to building them. Construction of an x-ray imaging system is expensive, therefore the ability to accurately model and optimize system configuration prior to building is very helpful.

Detector Characterization

I have characterized multix x-ray detectors including Redlen and Kromek photon-counting detectors as well as a ShadoBox energy integrating detector. Standard characterization measurements utilized were detector quantum efficiency, dark current, linearity, and energy resolution.
