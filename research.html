Research

X-Ray Phase Contrast Imaging

X-ray imaging is widely utilized to view the interior structure of objects. Transmission imaging, however, is generally unable to distinguish between similarly attenuationg materials. X-ray phase contrast imaging, which measures the change in phase through an object rather than the attenuation, can greatly improve material differentiation. Current phase imaging methods generally require complicated setups or expensive equipment. My work focuses on designing novel methods for phase imaging that are more accessible to aviation security and medical imaging applications.

Simulation

Simulation is a valuable tool for designing and evaluating the performance of x-ray imaging systems. Construction of an x-ray imaging system is expensive, but simulation provides us with the ability to accurately model and optimize system configuration prior to building.We use simulation to model a variety of x-ray imaging systems and explore the expected performance. One of the primary systems that I have explored is a hybrid CT+XRD imaging system was that was previously developed for improved identification of threat objects in checked baggage.

Photon Counting Detector Characterization

Photon counting detectors with energy resolving capabilities have the potential to improve computed tomography (CT) imaging and x-ray diffraction (XRD) systems. In order to better understand the use of these detectors in the CT and XRD application spaces, we have experimentally investigated the detector performance of two newly-released photon counting detectors: the Redlen LDA detector and the Kromek D-Matrix v2 detector.
